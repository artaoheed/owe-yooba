### Editorial Policy

Òwe Yòòbá is a living, community-contributed archive of Yoruba proverbs.
This policy describes how entries are sourced, translated, attributed,
corrected, and, when necessary, restricted.

**Sourcing.** Proverbs are folk expressions and are not copyrightable as
text. The archive draws from Wikiquote (Yoruba text only), personal and
elder contributions, and independent compilation from print sources
including Delano (1966), Ajibola (1947), Bamgbose, and Sheba. Bulk
reproduction from Owomoyela's *The Good Person* (2005) and from
yoruba.unl.edu is not undertaken pending permission from the University
of Nebraska-Lincoln.

**Translation.** Every English translation, literal or figurative, is
written independently by the archive's compilers. Copyrighted glosses
from named authors are not reproduced. Scholarly cross-references
appear in a `see also` field.

**Attribution.** Each entry names its source: a contributor, a
scholarly work cited as reference, or Wikiquote as text source.
Contributors are credited by public display name where they consent.

**Corrections.** Errors are inevitable. Corrections are welcomed at
`hello@oweyoruba.org` with subject line prefixed `[correction]`
followed by the entry slug. Accepted corrections are applied as git
commits; the full history is public.

**Disputed interpretations.** Where reputable sources disagree on
meaning, dialect, or usage, the archive records variants rather than
adjudicating between them. This preserves the plurality of the
tradition.

**Restricted knowledge.** Some proverbs sit close to Ifá divination,
ẹgbẹ́ associations, or other domains where public reproduction is
inappropriate without consultation. Any contributor or reviewer may
flag an entry as `restricted`. Restricted entries are excluded from
the public build. Publication follows consultation with knowledgeable
custodians of the tradition. Cultural-protocol labels in the manner
of Mukurtu are under consideration for the Phase 1 schema.

**Scope.** The archive covers proverbs (òwe) only. Words, names, and
general dictionary content are out of scope.

**License.** Original content is published under Creative Commons
Attribution 4.0 (CC BY 4.0).
